Lasagna recipe

Description

This quintessential comfort dish features layers of tender pasta sheets, a robust slow-simmered bolognese sauce, and a creamy three-cheese blend. The secret to a perfect structural integrity lies in the balance of the meat sauce and the herb-infused ricotta, ensuring each slice holds its shape while remaining incredibly moist. Topped with a generous layer of bubbly, golden-brown mozzarella and a sprinkle of fresh Italian parsley, it serves as the centerpiece for any family gathering or meal-prep routine.

The preparation process involves simmering ground beef with aromatics like garlic and onions before folding in crushed San Marzano tomatoes and a hint of dried oregano. While the sauce develops its deep flavor, the ricotta is whipped with an egg and fresh basil to create a light, velvety texture. Once assembled, the lasagna is baked until the edges are crispy and the center is piping hot. It is highly recommended to let the dish rest for at least fifteen minutes before slicing, allowing the layers to set and the flavors to fully meld together.

Ingredients

  • 12 lasagna noodles (oven-ready or par-boiled)
  • 1 lb lean ground beef
  • 2 cups shredded mozzarella cheese
  • 15 oz ricotta cheese
  • 1 jar (24 oz) marinara sauce
  • 1/2 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon dried oregano

Instructions

  1. Preheat your oven to 375 degrees.
  2. Brown the ground beef in a large skillet and drain the fat.
  3. Stir the marinara sauce into the beef and simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. In a small bowl, mix the ricotta cheese with the egg and basil. Layer the sauce, noodles, and cheese mixture in a baking dish
  5. Repeat the layers until all ingredients are used. Bake for 45 minutes or until the cheese is bubbly and brown.
  6. Let the lasagna rest for 10 minutes before cutting.
